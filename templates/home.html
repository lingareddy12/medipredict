Health Conditions

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood glucose levels (hyperglycemia) due to either inadequate insulin production or ineffective insulin utilization by the body. According to the International Diabetes Federation (IDF), as of 2021, an estimated 463 million adults (aged 20-79 years) were living with diabetes worldwide.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that develops in the cells of the breast. It occurs when abnormal cells in the breast grow and divide uncontrollably, forming a tumor. Breast cancer can affect both men and women, although it is much more common in women. According to the WHO, in 2020, there were an estimated 2.3 million new cases of breast cancer globally.

CardioVascular Diseases

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) refer to a group of disorders that affect the heart and blood vessels. They include conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart attacks, heart failure, stroke, and peripheral artery diseases. CVDs are the leading cause of death globally. According to the WHO, approximately 18 million deaths were attributed to CVDs in 2019.
